How to Dispose of Waste?

Bulk Waste Management

The Bulk Waste Drop-Off service offers Plainsboro residents a convenient option for disposing of items that aren't typically collected by their private waste hauler. The accepted items are listed on the left. Notably, commonly recycled items through curbside pickup, such as cardboard, plastic bottles/cans, and metal bottles/cans, are not accepted. Additionally, the facility does not accept construction and demolition debris; however, residents can dispose of small quantities of masonry and paving items. Residents must provide proof of residency before entering the Conservation Center. It's important to note that commercial vehicles are generally not allowed at the facility unless prior arrangements have been made with the Department of Public Works. F or more information, please contact the Department of Public Works at 609-799-0099.

Hazard Waste Management

The Middlesex County Division of Solid Waste Management offers county residents the opportunity to safely dispose of hazardous household items, such as drain cleaner, kerosene, paint thinner, etc., six times a year. The disposal of these items is free, and residents are required to leave the waste in the original container with the label intact, with a limit of 10 gallons per container. Only residential waste will be accepted during these collections, and commercial or industrial waste will not be accommodated. For detailed information on collection dates, locations, and accepted items, please visit the county website [link] or contact the Middlesex County Division of Solid Waste at 732-745-4170. This service is exclusively available to Middlesex County residents, and proof of residency (driver's license) is required. Note that the collection is limited to residential waste; no waste from commercial or industrial sources will be accepted.

Recycle Management

The Middlesex County Improvement Authority (MCIA) offers biweekly collection of recyclable materials for Plainsboro residents. To participate, residents must use the provided round blue container for commingled recyclables, including cans, bottles, plastic containers, cardboard, paper, and newspaper. Collection times are subject to change, so it's important to have all containers ready for pickup by 6 a.m. To ensure proper identification, the MCIA recommends residents write their name and address on each container using a permanent marker.
