Solid Waste Management in New Jersey

In the past, around 2010, New Jersey faced a significant challenge with approximately 22 million tons of solid waste generated, translating to about 5,000 pounds per person. Notably, the state successfully recycled 13.3 million tons, representing 60.5% of the total generated. In the accompanying pie chart, Middlesex County, encompassing Plainsboro, emerges as the second most recycled county in New Jersey. While Plainsboro is part of this accomplishment, it's essential to recognize the collective effort within this expansive county. To claim the title of the most recycled county in New Jersey, it is imperative for everyone to embrace proper recycling practices. Let's learn together and propel Middlesex County to the forefront of sustainable communities.

Slow Process

The increasing volume of waste is a major concern for humans and the environment. The best way to deal with this problem is to avoid products that generate waste materials that take more than a year to decompose in landfills. Every household and organization should also have a proactive plan for recycling to divert more materials away from the waste stream.
